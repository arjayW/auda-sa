19 /*
20 <b>How Audacity Redisplay Works \n
21  Roger Dannenberg</b> \n
22 Oct 2010 \n
23 
24 This is a brief guide to Audacity redisplay -- it may not be complete. It
25 is my attempt to understand the complicated graphics strategy.
26 
27 One basic idea is that redrawing waveforms is rather slow, so Audacity
28 saves waveform images in bitmaps to make redrawing faster. In particular,
29 during audio playback (and recording), the vertical time indicator is
30 drawn over the waveform about 20 times per second. To avoid unnecessary
31 computation, the indicator is erased by copying a column of pixels from
32 a bitmap image of the waveform. Notice that this implies a two-stage
33 process: first, waveforms are drawn to the bitmap; then, the bitmap
34 (or pieces of it) are copied to the screen, perhaps along with other
35 graphics.
36 
37 The bitmap is for the entire track panel, i.e. multiple tracks, and
38 includes things like the Gain and Pan slders to the left of the
39 waveform images.
40 
41 The screen update uses a mixture of direct drawing and indirect paint
42 events. The "normal" way to update a graphical display is to call
43 the Refresh() method when something invalidates the screen. Later, the
44 system calls OnPaint(), which the application overrides to (re)draw the
45 screen. In wxWidgets, you can also draw directly to the screen without
46 calling Refresh() and without waiting for OnPaint() to be called.
47 
48 I would expect there to be a 2-level invalidation scheme: Some changes
49 invalidate the bitmap, forcing a bitmap redraw *and* a screen redraw.
50 Other changes merely update the screen using pre-existing bitmaps. In
51 Audacity, the "2-level" invalidation works like this: Anything
52 that invalidates the bitmap calls TrackPanel::Refresh(), which
53 has an eraseBackground parameter. This flag says to redraw the
54 bitmap when OnPaint() is called. If eraseBackground is false, the
55 existing bitmap can be used for waveform images. Audacity also
56 draws directly to the screen to update the time indicator during
57 playback. To move the indicator, one column of pixels is drawn to
58 the screen to remove the indicator. Then the indicator is drawn at
59 a NEW time location.
60 
61 Notice that the zoom guidelines, the focused track highlight,
62 and snap guidelines could be drawn directly to the screen rather than to
63 the bitmap, generally eliminating redraw work.
64 
65 One problem is slider udpates. Sliders are in the left area of the track
66 panel. They are not wxWindows like wxSliders, but instead are just drawn
67 on the TrackPanel. When slider state changes, *all* tracks do a full
68 refresh, including recomputing the backing store. It would make more sense
69 to just invalidate the region containing the slider. However, doing that
70 would require either incrementally updating the bitmap (not currently done),
71 or maintaining the sliders and other track info on the screen and not in
72 the bitmap.
73 
74 */
